    Worksheet LT 1: Sketching Qualitative Graphs
    

    Objectives

    • Sketch a real-life scenario
      🔗
      🔗
    • Label the graph with appropriate variable definitions
      🔗
      🔗
    🔗

    Definition 1.

    A qualitative graph is a graph that represents the relationship and general trend between two quantities without using specific numerical values or measurements">
    🔗
    🔗

    Definition 2.

    An independent variable is an input variable whose value can be chosen freely and does not depend on any other variable in the problem or function. It is the variable that you change or manipulate to see how it affects another variable.
    🔗
    🔗

    Definition 3.

    A dependent variable is the output variable whose value changes in response to, or depends on, the value of the independent variable. It represents the outcome or the result that you are measuring or calculating in a function or problem.
    🔗
    🔗

    1.

    Aiden and Elijah are canoing. At first they paddle quickly away from the dock, they tire and rest for a few minutes, then they paddle back to show, slower than before.
    🔗

    (a)

    What two things are being related in this story?
    🔗
    Answer.
    time or minutes and distance away from the dock.
    🔗
    🔗
    🔗

    (b)

    Which in the dependent variable?
    🔗
    Answer.
    The dependent variable is the one that can be measured. We are measuring the distance away from the dock.
    🔗
    🔗
    🔗

    (c)

    Sketch the graph of the story. Be sure to label your axes with the appropriate definitions.
