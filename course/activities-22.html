    Worksheet LT 12 Exponentials and Logs as Inverses
    

    Objectives

    • Recognize exponentials and log graphs as inverses
      🔗
      🔗
    • Convert between exponentials and logs (both ways).
      🔗
      🔗
    • Change the base of a logatithm to base 10 or a natural base.
      🔗
      🔗
    🔗

    1.

    Reveiwing Inverses:
    🔗
    We know that the domain of \(f(x)\) is the _____________ of \(f^{-1}(x)\text{.}\)
    🔗
    We know that the range of \(f(x)\) is the ____________ of \(f^{-1}(x)\text{.}\)
    🔗
    We know if \(f^{-1}(x)\) contains the point (3, -7), then the inverse point on \(f(x)\) is (_____,_____).
    🔗
    We know that inverses "undo" each other. Such as addition undoing ________ and division undoing ___________.
    🔗
    🔗

    2.

    We will be graphing equations in Desmos, and creating a rough sketch on the grid provided. Enter the equations given and determine if they are inverse equations. Always have the line \(y=x\) graphed.
    🔗

    (a)

    Graph \(y=x+5\) and \(y=x-5\text{.}\) Are the two equations inverses? Explain your thinking.
    🔗
    🔗

    (b)

    Graph \(y=9x+1\) and \(y=9x-1\text{.}\) Are the two equations inverses? Explain your thinking.
    🔗
    🔗

    (c)

    Graph \(y=10^x\) and \(\text{.}\) log(x) Are the two equations inverses? Explain your thinking.
    🔗
    🔗

    (d)

    Graph \(y=4^x\) and \(y=log_4(x)\text{.}\)(write the equation y=log_4(x)) Are the two equations inverses? Explain your thinking.
    🔗
    🔗

    (e)

    Graph \(y=e^x\) and \(y=ln(x)\text{.}\) Are the two equations inverses? Explain your thinking.
    🔗
    🔗
