    Worksheet LT 4 Linear Graphs
    

    Objectives

    • Identify the slope.
      🔗
    • Identify the intercepts.
      🔗
    • Evaluate \(f(a)\) and find \(x\) when given \(f(a)\text{.}\)
      🔗
    • Make the equation of the line.
      🔗
    🔗

    Definition 4.

    Slope is the measure of the steepness and direction of a line. It is calculated as the ratio of the vertical change ("rise") to the horizontal change ("run") between any two distinct points on a line.
    🔗
    🔗

    Definition 5.

    An intercept is the point where a line or curve crosses or intersects an axis on a coordinate plane.
    🔗
    🔗

    1.

    Finding Slope
    🔗

    (a)

    How will you find the vertical change and the horizontal change?
    🔗
    Answer.
    For the vertical change, find the difference of the y values. For the horizontal change, find the difference of the x values.
    🔗
    🔗
    🔗

    (b)

    Find the slope of the line between the two points labeled on the graph. Be sure to show your work.
    🔗
    Answer.
    Using points \((1,-1)\) and \((4,2)\) from the graph:
    \begin{align*} \frac{2-(-1)}{4-1} \amp= \frac{2+1}{3}\\ \amp= \frac{3}{3}\\ \amp= 1 \end{align*}
    🔗
    The slope of the line is \(1\text{.}\)
    🔗
    🔗
    🔗
    🔗

    2. Intercepts of Linear Graphs.

    In general terms, what is the correct ordered pair for the y-intercept, \((a,0)\) or \((0,b)\text{?}\) Explain your reasoning.
    🔗
    Answer.
    The correct ordered pair is \((0,b)\text{.}\) The x-value is \(0\) because to graph a y-intercept you start at the origin and do not move left or right, so \(x=0\text{,}\) then move up or down to the point \((0,b)\text{.}\)
    🔗
    🔗
    🔗

    3.

    Finding Intercepts
    🔗

    (a)

    What is the y-intercept of the graph?
    🔗
    Answer.
    The point that intersects the y-axis, or the y-intercept, is \((0,-2)\text{.}\)
    🔗
    🔗
    🔗

    (b)

    What is the x-intercept of the graph?
    🔗
    Answer.
    The point that intersects the x-axis, or the x-intercept, is \((2,0)\text{.}\)
    🔗
    🔗
    🔗
    🔗

    Definition 6.

    Function notation is a way to write a function using symbols that show the relationship between an input and an output. Notation: \(f(x)=y\)
    🔗
    • \(f\) — the name of the function.
      🔗
    • \(x\) — the input of the function (also called the independent variable).
      🔗
    • \(f(x)\) — the output when \(x\) is applied to the function.
      🔗
    🔗
    🔗

    4.

    Using Function Notation on the Graph of \(f(x)\text{.}\)
    🔗

    (a)

    Explain how you would use the graph to find \(f(3)\text{.}\)
    🔗
    Answer.
    Go to \(3\) on the x-axis since we know that \(x=3\text{.}\) Go up or down from there until you reach the line. The y-value at that point is your answer.
    🔗
    🔗
    🔗

    (b)

    Use the graph to find \(f(3)\text{.}\)
    🔗
    Answer.
    The point where \(x=3\) is \((3,1)\text{.}\) This tells us \(f(3)=1\text{.}\)
    🔗
    🔗
    🔗

    (c)

    Explain how you would use the graph to find \(x\) when \(f(x)=-4\text{.}\)
    🔗
    Answer.
    Go to \(-4\) on the y-axis since we know that \(y=-4\text{.}\) Go left or right from there until you reach the line. The x-value at that point is your answer.
    🔗
    🔗
    🔗

    (d)

    Use the graph to find \(x\) when \(f(x)=-4\text{.}\)
    🔗
    Answer.
    The point where \(y=-4\) is \((-2,-4)\text{.}\) This tells us \(x=-2\) when \(f(x)=-4\text{.}\)
    🔗
